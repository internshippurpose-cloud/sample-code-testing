What is a computer?

A computer is an electronic device that takes raw data as input, processes it using software instructions, and produces meaningful information as output, also storing it for future use.
It performs arithmetic and logical operations at high speeds, functioning through hardware (physical parts like keyboard, CPU) and software (programs and instructions) to execute a wide range of tasks, from calculations to creating documents and browsing the web.
Everything you do on your computer will rely on both hardware and software.
For example, right now you may be viewing this lesson in a web browser (software) and using your mouse (hardware) to click from page to page.
As you learn about different types of computers, ask yourself about the differences in their hardware.
As you progress through this tutorial, you'll see that different types of computers also often use different types of software.
